Glossary

This glossary explains important terms and abbreviations you'll encounter during the international protection process in Ireland. Click on any letter below to jump to that section.

A

Accommodation Centre
A place where people seeking international protection can live while their application is being processed. Also known as Direct Provision centres.
Appeal
The process of asking a higher authority to review a decision about your application. You have the right to appeal negative decisions to the International Protection Appeals Tribunal (IPAT).
Asylum
Protection given by a country to someone fleeing persecution in their home country. See also: International Protection

B

Biometrics
Your fingerprints and photograph taken during registration. These help confirm your identity throughout the process.
Breach
Breaking a rule or not following a requirement. For example, not reporting to GNIB when required could be considered a breach of your conditions.

C

Case Worker
The IPO official assigned to review your application and conduct your interview.
COI (Country of Origin Information)
Official information about conditions in your home country used to assess your application.
Credibility
How believable your story is. The IPO will assess whether your account of events is credible when making their decision.

D

Deportation Order
An official order requiring you to leave Ireland. This is issued if your application and appeals are unsuccessful.
Direct Provision
The system of accommodation and basic services provided to people seeking international protection in Ireland. See also: Accommodation Centre

E

EURODAC
A European database of fingerprints used to identify if you have made an application in another EU country.
Examination
The process of reviewing your application, including your interview and all supporting documents.

F

Family Reunification
The right to bring certain family members to join you in Ireland if you receive international protection.
First Instance Decision
The initial decision made by the IPO on your application, before any appeals.

G

GNIB (Garda National Immigration Bureau)
The police unit that handles immigration matters. You must register with them if required. Now part of ISD (Immigration Service Delivery).
Geneva Convention
The 1951 international agreement that defines who is a refugee and outlines their rights.

H

Habitual Residence
A condition you must meet to access certain social welfare benefits. It means you have been living in Ireland for a certain time and intend to stay.
Humanitarian Leave to Remain
Permission to stay in Ireland granted by the Minister for Justice on humanitarian grounds, even if you don't qualify for international protection.

I

IPO (International Protection Office)
The government office that processes applications for international protection in Ireland. Located at 79-83 Lower Mount Street, Dublin 2.
IPAT (International Protection Appeals Tribunal)
The independent body that hears appeals against negative decisions from the IPO.
International Protection
The official term for refugee status and subsidiary protection in Ireland. This replaced the older term "asylum".
Interpreter
A person who translates spoken communication between languages. You have the right to an interpreter during your interview if needed.

L

Legal Aid
Free or low-cost legal help. The Legal Aid Board provides lawyers to help with your international protection application.
Leave to Remain
Permission to stay in Ireland legally. Different types include humanitarian leave, permission to remain, and leave based on international protection.

M

Medical Card
A card that gives you free access to most health services in Ireland. Most international protection applicants are entitled to a medical card.
Minor
A person under 18 years of age. Unaccompanied minors receive special protection and support. See also: Unaccompanied Minor

N

Non-refoulement
The principle that protects you from being returned to a country where you face serious threats to your life or freedom.
Notice of Appeal
The formal document you submit to appeal a negative decision. You usually have 15 working days to submit this.

O

Oral Hearing
An in-person appeal hearing before the IPAT where you can present your case and answer questions.
Own Door Accommodation
Independent housing instead of living in an accommodation centre. Some applicants may be eligible for this after a certain period.

P

Permission to Remain
Legal permission to stay in Ireland for a specific period. This can be granted for various reasons including international protection.
Persecution
Serious harm or threats because of your race, religion, nationality, political opinion, or membership of a particular social group.
PPS Number
Personal Public Service Number - a unique reference number for accessing public services in Ireland. You'll receive this after registering your application.

R

Refugee Status
Protection granted if you cannot return to your country due to persecution based on race, religion, nationality, political opinion, or membership of a particular social group.
Reception Conditions
The standards of accommodation, food, healthcare, and other services that must be provided to international protection applicants.
Reporting
The requirement to regularly present yourself to immigration authorities or Gardaí as directed.

S

Safe Country
A country considered safe for returns. If you traveled through or have connections to a safe country, this may affect your application.
Subsidiary Protection
Protection granted if you don't qualify as a refugee but would face serious harm if returned to your country.
Single Procedure
The system where applications for refugee status, subsidiary protection, and permission to remain are decided together.

T

TRC (Temporary Residence Certificate)
The identity document issued to international protection applicants. You must carry this at all times and renew it as required.
Transfer
Moving between accommodation centres, or being sent to another EU country under Dublin Regulation rules.
Translation
Converting written documents from one language to another. Important documents not in English or Irish must be translated.

U

Unaccompanied Minor
A person under 18 who arrives in Ireland without a parent or guardian. They receive special care and protection from TUSLA.
UNHCR
United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees - the UN agency that protects refugees worldwide.

V

Voluntary Return
Choosing to return to your home country with assistance from IOM (International Organization for Migration).
Vulnerable Person
Someone who needs special care, such as unaccompanied minors, elderly people, pregnant women, or people with serious illnesses.

W

Withdrawal
Officially stopping your application for international protection. This is a serious decision that cannot easily be reversed.
Work Permit
Permission to work in Ireland. International protection applicants can apply for this after 5 months if no first instance decision has been made.
