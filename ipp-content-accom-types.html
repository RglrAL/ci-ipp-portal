Introduction

When you apply for international protection in Ireland, you will be offered accommodation while your application is being processed. There are different types of accommodation available, and the type you're offered depends on availability and your family situation.

Video: Overview of accommodation options for international protection applicants

All accommodation provided is temporary while your protection application is being assessed. This page explains the different types of accommodation and what you can expect in each.

The International Protection Accommodation Services (IPAS) manages accommodation for people seeking international protection. They work to ensure all residents have safe, suitable accommodation that meets their needs.

Direct Provision centres

Direct Provision centres are the most common type of accommodation for protection applicants. These centres provide:

  • A bedroom (single or shared)
  • Three meals a day
  • Basic toiletries and household items
  • Access to laundry facilities
  • Common areas for socialising

Typical Direct Provision facilities

Centres vary in size and facilities. Some are purpose-built, while others are converted hotels or guesthouses. All centres must meet minimum standards set by the government.

In Direct Provision, you receive a weekly allowance called the Daily Expenses Allowance. This is currently €38.80 per adult and €29.80 per child per week.

Weekly allowance rates for Direct Provision residents

Emergency accommodation

Due to high demand, some people are initially placed in emergency accommodation. This might be in:

  • Hotels or B&Bs
  • Emergency reception centres
  • Temporary facilities

Important: Temporary nature of emergency accommodation

Emergency accommodation is a very short-term solution. You should expect to be moved to more permanent accommodation within a few weeks.

Emergency accommodation is meant to be very short-term while more suitable accommodation is found. Facilities may be more limited than in established centres.

If you're in emergency accommodation, you should be moved to more permanent accommodation as soon as possible. Keep in regular contact with IPAS about your situation.

Own door accommodation

Own door accommodation means having your own self-contained unit, such as:

  • An apartment
  • A house
  • Self-catering unit within a centre

Comparison of Direct Provision vs Own Door accommodation

In own door accommodation, you:

  • Cook your own meals
  • Manage your own household
  • Receive a higher weekly allowance
  • Have more privacy and independence

Interactive map: Own door accommodation locations (click to explore)

Not everyone can access own door accommodation immediately. Priority is usually given to families with children and people with specific needs.

Your rights and standards

Regardless of your accommodation type, you have the right to:

  • Safe, clean accommodation
  • Privacy and dignity
  • Access to healthcare and education
  • Practice your religion
  • Make complaints if standards aren't met

Your rights in accommodation - visual guide

The National Standards for accommodation centres set out what you should expect. These cover areas like living space, food quality, facilities, and staff conduct.

If you have concerns about your accommodation, you can:

  • Speak to the centre manager
  • Contact IPAS directly
  • Make a formal complaint
  • Contact support organisations for help

Transfers and moves

You might need to move accommodation for various reasons:

  • Centre closures or renovations
  • Family reunification
  • Medical or educational needs
  • Safety concerns

IPAS manages all transfers. You can request a transfer, but approval depends on availability and your circumstances. Emergency transfers may be arranged in cases of serious concern.

How the transfer process works - step by step

If you're being transferred:

  • You'll receive advance notice (except in emergencies)
  • Transport will be arranged if needed
  • Your children's education should be considered
  • Medical needs will be taken into account
