Medical card

As an international protection applicant, you are entitled to a medical card. This card gives you free access to most healthcare services in Ireland.

Your medical card covers:

  • GP (doctor) visits
  • Hospital services
  • Prescription medicines
  • Some dental and optical services

You should receive your medical card within a few weeks of registering. If you haven't received it, contact the accommodation centre manager or your social worker.

GP services

A GP (General Practitioner) is a family doctor who provides primary healthcare. With your medical card, GP visits are free.

To see a GP:

  • Register with a local GP who accepts medical cards
  • Call to make an appointment
  • Bring your medical card to every visit

Your accommodation centre can help you find a GP in your area.

Hospital care

Hospital services are free with your medical card. This includes:

  • Emergency department visits
  • Outpatient appointments
  • Inpatient stays
  • Maternity services

For emergencies, call 999 or 112. For non-emergencies, you usually need a referral from your GP.

Prescriptions

With a medical card, most prescription medicines cost €1.50 per item, up to a maximum of €15 per month per person.

To get prescription medicines:

  • Get a prescription from your GP
  • Take it to any pharmacy
  • Show your medical card
  • Pay the prescription charge

Dental care

Emergency dental treatment is free with a medical card. Routine dental care has limited coverage.

Free dental services include:

  • Oral examinations
  • Emergency pain relief
  • Extractions when necessary
  • Some fillings

You need approval for some treatments. Ask your dentist about what's covered.

Mental health support

Mental health services are available and important for your wellbeing. Support includes:

  • Counselling services
  • Psychology appointments
  • Psychiatric care if needed
  • Support groups

Your GP can refer you to mental health services. Some organisations also provide free counselling for asylum seekers.
