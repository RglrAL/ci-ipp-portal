First steps and registration

How to apply

To apply for international protection in Ireland, you must present yourself to the International Protection Office (IPO). You cannot apply by post, email, or through someone else.

You should apply as soon as possible after arriving in Ireland. The application is free - there are no fees at any stage of the process.

Where to register

You must register your application at:

International Protection Office (IPO)
79-83 Lower Mount Street
Dublin 2
D02 ND99

The office is open Monday to Friday. You should arrive early as there may be queues, especially on Mondays.

Your first day

On your first day at the IPO, you will:

  • Have your photograph taken
  • Provide your fingerprints
  • Complete initial registration forms
  • Receive information about the process
  • Be offered accommodation if needed

The process usually takes several hours. Interpreters are available if you need one.

Temporary Residence Certificate

After registering, you'll receive a Temporary Residence Certificate (TRC). This important document:

  • Shows you have permission to remain in Ireland
  • Contains your personal details and photograph
  • Has a unique reference number
  • Must be renewed regularly

Always carry your TRC with you. You'll need it to access services and prove your identity.
