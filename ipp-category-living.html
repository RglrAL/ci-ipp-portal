Life in Ireland

Building a new life in Ireland involves understanding how essential services work, connecting with your community, and embracing Irish culture. This comprehensive guide provides practical information to help you navigate daily life and access the support you need.

From healthcare and education to employment rights and cultural activities, discover the resources available to help you integrate into Irish society and build a fulfilling life in your new home.

Healthcare services

Accessing GP services, hospitals, medical cards, prescriptions, dental care, mental health support, and understanding the Irish healthcare system.

Education system

Understanding Irish schools, enrolling children, third-level education, adult education courses, language classes, and educational support services.

Employment and work

Work permits, finding employment, workers' rights, minimum wage, tax system, social insurance, and career development opportunities in Ireland.

Housing and accommodation

Finding private rental accommodation, understanding tenancy rights, HAP scheme, social housing, and dealing with landlords in Ireland.

Money and benefits

Social welfare payments, child benefit, opening bank accounts, managing finances, understanding taxes, and accessing financial support.

Transport and travel

Public transport systems, Leap cards, driving licenses, cycling, travel within Ireland, and accessing transport support schemes.

Irish culture and society

Understanding Irish customs, festivals, sports (GAA), music, arts, local community groups, and ways to connect with Irish culture.

Legal matters and citizenship

Understanding Irish law, accessing legal services, path to citizenship, naturalisation process, and your legal rights in Ireland.

Community and support

Finding community organisations, religious services, cultural associations, volunteer opportunities, and building your support network in Ireland.
