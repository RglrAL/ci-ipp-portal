The application process

Applying for international protection in Ireland is a structured process designed to assess your need for refugee status or subsidiary protection. This step-by-step guide will help you understand each stage of your application, from initial registration to the final decision.

Learn about required documents, interview preparation, your rights during the process, and what to expect at each stage. Understanding the process helps you provide the information needed for a fair assessment of your application.

First steps and registration

How to apply for international protection, where to register, what happens on your first day, and receiving your Temporary Residence Certificate (TRC).

Required documents

Essential documents needed for your application, how to obtain missing documents, translation requirements, and keeping your paperwork organized.

The IPO questionnaire

Understanding and completing the International Protection Office questionnaire, getting help with forms, and submitting your application correctly.

Interview preparation

What to expect during your protection interview, how to prepare, your rights during the interview, interpreters, and getting legal advice.

Your rights and entitlements

Understanding your legal rights during the application process, access to legal aid, confidentiality, and protection while your case is being assessed.

Decisions and outcomes

Understanding IPO decisions, types of protection status, what happens after a positive decision, and next steps following your determination.

Appeals process

How to appeal a negative decision, understanding the International Protection Appeals Tribunal, timelines, legal representation, and appeal hearings.

Family reunification

Applying to bring family members to Ireland, eligibility criteria, required documentation, and the family reunification application process.

Help and support

Where to get help with your application, legal aid services, support organizations, interpreters, and assistance for vulnerable applicants.
