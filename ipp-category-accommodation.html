Living in an accommodation centre

Finding a place to live is crucial for your well-being as you await a decision on your application for international protection. Whether you're in Direct Provision, Emergency Accommodation, or other housing arrangements, this section provides essential information about your rights, daily life, and available support services.

Learn about meals, healthcare access, education opportunities, and how to connect with your local community while living in accommodation centres across Ireland.

Accommodation types

Understand the different types of accommodation available including Direct Provision centres, emergency accommodation, and own-door accommodation options.

Daily living essentials

Information about meals, laundry facilities, communal areas, visiting hours, and the daily allowance you receive while in accommodation.

Healthcare access

How to register with a GP, access medical cards, emergency healthcare, mental health support, and specialist services while in accommodation.

Education and children

Enrolling children in schools, accessing early childhood care, school transport, uniforms, books, and additional educational support services.

Rights and responsibilities

Your rights as a resident, house rules, complaints procedures, transfers between centres, and how to report issues or concerns.

Community integration

Connecting with local communities, accessing sports and recreation, religious services, cultural activities, and volunteer opportunities.

Support services

Access to social workers, interpreters, legal aid, advocacy services, and specialised support for vulnerable individuals and families.

Moving to own accommodation

Information about transitioning out of Direct Provision, finding private rental accommodation, and understanding your housing options.

Emergency situations

What to do in emergencies, accessing crisis support, reporting safety concerns, and understanding evacuation procedures in your centre.
